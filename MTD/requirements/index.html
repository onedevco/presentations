1. Overview & Eligibility

Making Tax Digital for Income Tax Self Assessment (MTD ITSA) requires landlords to keep digital records and submit quarterly updates to HMRC using compatible software. This replaces the traditional annual Self Assessment tax return for property income.

Who Must Comply

MTD ITSA applies to individuals with qualifying income from:

  • UK property rental income
  • Foreign property rental income
  • Self-employment income (combined with property income for threshold calculation)

Qualifying Income Definition

Qualifying income is the gross income (before expenses) from self-employment and/or property. This is not your taxable profit - it's total turnover.

Income Thresholds

Mandatory From Qualifying Income Threshold Based On Tax Year
6 April 2026 Over ¬£50,000 2024-25
6 April 2027 Over ¬£30,000 2025-26
6 April 2028 Over ¬£20,000 2026-27

Multiple Income Sources

If you have both property income AND self-employment income, the gross amounts are combined to determine if you exceed the threshold. Each business type requires separate quarterly submissions.

3. Quarterly Update Requirements

Quarterly updates are cumulative summaries of your property business income and expenses submitted every three months. These are not tax calculations - they are simply records of transactions.

What Quarterly Updates Contain

  • Cumulative income - Total rental income received year-to-date
  • Cumulative expenses - Total allowable expenses year-to-date
  • Category breakdowns - Income and expenses split by HMRC-defined categories

Cumulative Submissions

Each quarterly update includes all income and expenses from the start of the tax year, not just that quarter. This means Q2 includes Q1+Q2 totals, Q3 includes Q1+Q2+Q3, etc. Corrections can be made in subsequent submissions.

Separate Submissions Required

You must submit separate quarterly updates for:

  • UK Property - All UK rental properties combined into one submission
  • Foreign Property - All overseas rental properties combined into one submission
  • Self-Employment - Each self-employed business separately (if applicable)

Example: Landlord with Multiple Properties

If you own 3 UK rental properties and 1 property in Spain:

  • 1 quarterly update for UK Property (all 3 UK properties combined)
  • 1 quarterly update for Foreign Property (Spanish property)

Total: 8 quarterly submissions per year (4 √ó 2 property types)

Standard vs Calendar Quarters

Quarter Standard Period Calendar Quarter (Optional) Deadline
Q1 6 April - 5 July 1 April - 30 June 7 August
Q2 6 July - 5 October 1 July - 30 September 7 November
Q3 6 October - 5 January 1 October - 31 December 7 February
Q4 6 January - 5 April 1 January - 31 March 7 May

Note: Calendar quarter election must be made in advance and applies to all income sources.

4. Income Data Fields (UK Property)

The following fields are used when submitting UK property income via the HMRC Property Business API. All monetary values must be between 0 and 99999999999.99 with up to 2 decimal places.

Income Object Fields

API Field Name Description SA105 Box Status
totalRentsReceived Total rents and other income from property. The total amount of rental income received for the period. Box 5 Required
premiumsOfLeaseGrant Premiums received for granting a lease. Taxable portion of any lease premium received. Box 6 Optional
reversePremiums Reverse premiums received as an inducement to take on a lease. Box 7 Optional
otherPropertyIncome Other income from property not included elsewhere (e.g., income from land). Box 8 Optional
rentARoom.amountClaimed Amount claimed under the Rent a Room scheme (max ¬£7,500 per year). - Conditional

Rent a Room Scheme

If you rent out a furnished room in your home and receive less than ¬£7,500 per year, you can use the Rent a Room scheme. If using this, include the rentARoom.amountClaimed field with the amount you're claiming relief on.

Example Income Payload

"income": { "totalRentsReceived": 12000.00, "premiumsOfLeaseGrant": 0, "reversePremiums": 0, "otherPropertyIncome": 500.00 }

5. Expense Data Fields (UK Property)

You have two options for submitting expenses:

  1. Consolidated Expenses - A single total figure (simpler, for turnover under ¬£90,000)
  2. Itemised Expenses - Broken down by category (required for full expense claims)

Choose One Method

You cannot mix consolidated and itemised expenses. If you use consolidatedExpenses, you cannot also submit individual expense categories in the same submission.

Option 1: Consolidated Expenses

API Field Name Description Status
consolidatedExpenses The sum of all allowable expenses for the specified period. Use this for simplified "three-line accounts" reporting. Either/Or

Option 2: Itemised Expenses

API Field Name Description SA105 Box
premisesRunningCosts Rent, rates, insurance, ground rents and other costs of running the property. Box 9
repairsAndMaintenance Property repairs and maintenance costs (not improvements). Box 10
financialCosts Loan interest and other financial costs (non-residential property only). Box 11
professionalFees Legal, management and other professional fees. Box 12
costOfServices Cost of services provided, including wages (e.g., gardener, cleaner). Box 13
travelCosts Car, van and travel costs incurred for property business. Box 14
other Other allowable property expenses not covered above. Box 15
residentialFinancialCost Residential property finance costs (mortgage interest for residential lets - special rules apply). Box 44
residentialFinancialCostsCarriedForward Residential finance costs brought forward from previous years. Box 45

Residential Finance Cost Restriction

For residential property, mortgage interest relief is restricted to a 20% tax credit. You must enter the full finance cost in residentialFinancialCost rather than financialCosts. HMRC will calculate the tax credit automatically.

Example Itemised Expenses Payload

"expenses": { "premisesRunningCosts": 1200.00, "repairsAndMaintenance": 850.00, "financialCosts": 0, "professionalFees": 600.00, "costOfServices": 0, "travelCosts": 150.00, "other": 200.00, "residentialFinancialCost": 4500.00 }

6. End of Period Statement (EOPS)

The End of Period Statement is submitted after the tax year ends. It finalises your property business figures and makes any necessary adjustments.

Purpose of EOPS

  • Confirm the accuracy of quarterly submissions
  • Make year-end adjustments (e.g., accruals, prepayments)
  • Apply capital allowances (if applicable)
  • Confirm that figures are complete and correct

EOPS Requirements

Aspect Requirement
When to Submit After 5 April (end of tax year) and before 31 January
Separate Submissions One EOPS per business type (UK Property, Foreign Property, each Self-Employment)
Deadline 31 January following the end of the tax year

Adjustments in EOPS

The EOPS allows you to make adjustments that weren't required in quarterly updates:

  • Accounting adjustments - Accruals, prepayments, closing stock
  • Capital allowances - Annual Investment Allowance, balancing charges
  • Loss relief - Brought forward losses, sideways relief claims
  • Private use adjustments - Disallowable portions for personal use

No Adjustments in Quarterly Updates

Quarterly updates are raw transaction summaries. You don't need to worry about accruals, capital allowances, or tax adjustments until the EOPS stage.

7. Final Declaration

The Final Declaration replaces the traditional Self Assessment tax return. It confirms your total tax liability across all income sources.

What the Final Declaration Covers

  • All property income (from EOPS submissions)
  • Employment income (from P60/P45)
  • Savings and investment income
  • Dividend income
  • Capital gains
  • Pension contributions and Gift Aid
  • Other taxable income

Single Declaration

You only submit one Final Declaration per tax year, regardless of how many property businesses or employments you have. This covers ALL your income.

Final Declaration Process

  1. Complete all quarterly updates for each business
  2. Submit EOPS for each business
  3. Add any other income sources (employment, savings, etc.)
  4. Review calculated tax liability
  5. Submit Final Declaration through MTD software

Key Difference from Self Assessment

The Final Declaration must be submitted through MTD-compatible software. You cannot file through HMRC's online Self Assessment portal. The software submits directly via HMRC's API.

Grace Period

Quarterly updates are due by the 7th day of the month following the deadline month (e.g., Q1 ends 5 July, due by 7 August - giving you about a month to submit).

9. Digital Record-Keeping Requirements

What Must Be Recorded Digitally

  • Date of transaction
  • Amount of income or expense
  • Category (rent, repairs, insurance, etc.)
  • Description/narrative (optional but recommended)

What Does NOT Need to Be Digital

  • Physical receipts and invoices (keep paper copies)
  • Bank statements (the digital record just needs the key data)
  • Contracts and tenancy agreements

Digital Link Requirement

There must be a digital link between your records and the submission to HMRC. This means no manual re-keying of totals. Your spreadsheet or software must connect (directly or via bridging software) to the HMRC API.

Acceptable Record-Keeping Methods

Method Compliant? Notes
MTD-compatible accounting software ‚úÖ Yes Direct submission via API
Spreadsheet + bridging software ‚úÖ Yes Spreadsheet must link digitally to bridging tool
Custom Next.js application ‚úÖ Yes Must submit via HMRC API with fraud headers
Paper records only ‚ùå No Not compliant - must be digital
Spreadsheet with manual submission ‚ùå No Breaks digital link requirement

Record Retention

Records must be kept for 5 years after the 31 January submission deadline for the relevant tax year. For tax year 2026-27, records must be kept until at least 31 January 2033.

10. Penalty Regime

HMRC is introducing a new points-based penalty system for MTD ITSA from April 2026.

Late Submission Penalties

Penalty Points Consequence
1 point Warning - no financial penalty
2 points Warning - no financial penalty
3 points Warning - no financial penalty
4 points (threshold) ¬£200 penalty + ¬£200 for each subsequent late submission

Points Expiry

Penalty points expire after 24 months of compliant submissions. Points are tracked per obligation type (quarterly updates vs annual submissions).

Late Payment Penalties

Days Overdue Penalty
1-15 days No penalty (grace period)
16-30 days 2% of tax outstanding at day 15
31+ days 2% of tax at day 15 + 2% of tax at day 30
Ongoing Additional 4% per annum on outstanding balance

11. API Endpoints Reference

The following HMRC APIs are relevant for landlord property submissions:

Property Business API (v6.0)

POST /individuals/business/property/{nino}/{businessId}/period/{taxYear}

Create and Amend a UK Property Cumulative Period Summary (quarterly update)

GET /individuals/business/property/{nino}/{businessId}/period/{taxYear}

Retrieve a UK Property Cumulative Period Summary

PUT /individuals/business/property/{nino}/{businessId}/annual/{taxYear}

Create and Amend a UK Property Business Annual Submission

Obligations API

GET /obligations/details/{nino}/income-and-expenditure

Retrieve Income Tax (Self Assessment) Income and Expenditure Obligations

Business Details API (v2.0)

GET /individuals/business/details/{nino}/list

List All Businesses - retrieve your businessId for property submissions

Individual Calculations API

POST /individuals/calculations/{nino}/self-assessment/{taxYear}

Trigger a Self Assessment Tax Calculation

Individuals Business EOPS API (v3.0)

POST /individuals/business/end-of-period-statement/{nino}

Submit End of Period Statement for a Business

Fraud Prevention Headers Required

All API calls must include legally-required fraud prevention headers. These include device identifiers, connection method, timestamps, and other metadata. Failure to provide accurate headers will result in rejection of production access.
